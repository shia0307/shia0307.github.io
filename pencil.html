Project Summary

Reborned Pencil is a project I worked on in 2012. My teammates and I participated in the National College Industrial Design Competition, and won the Award of Excellence in Guangdong Regional.

Reborned Pencil is a pencil redesign concept that extent the possible use of a single pencil. This new pencil introduces no residua and teaches children basic concept of color and plant in a fun an easy way.

Too Many And Too Short

The pencils we use now are made of wood, when pencils being sharpened, it introduce a lot of pencil scraps. Also, the short stubs of pencil are really hard to use. All there made us thinking,

"What if we can get the most possible use out of every pencil?

Reborned Pencil

We want to help pencils to be borned again when we are done with writing and drawing. We put plants seed in the end of every pencil, when finished, you can plant them in the soil, and they will grow beautiful flowers and plants as you choose.

We designed this specifically for children around 3-8 years old. At this age, they start to enjoy drawing and learn the concept of color. We want them to enjoy the drawing process with educational purpose. Parents can also teach them sense of responsibility by helping them take care of the new plant.

Material and Sustainability

It's very important to note that reborned pencil is entirely non-toxis and made of all natural material. Bagasse is the residua of sugarcane, it is the perfect material for reborned pencil, because it is has rich nutrition remained in it, and it can be served as fertilizer for the other growing plants. Also, with a little bit of pressure, it is strong enought to hold up together to make a working pencil.
